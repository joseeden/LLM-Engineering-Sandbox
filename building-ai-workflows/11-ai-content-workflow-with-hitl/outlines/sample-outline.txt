Title: Why Most Marathon Runners Fail Before Race Day

Angle:
Many runners believe race day is the hardest part of a marathon, but most failures happen during training because of poor planning, unrealistic expectations, and inconsistent habits.

Key ideas:

* Marathon training is a long-term process.
* Consistency is more important than occasional hard workouts.
* Recovery is a critical part of training.
* Many runners increase mileage too quickly.
* Nutrition and hydration affect performance.
* Mental preparation matters as much as physical preparation.
* Small habits repeated over time lead to better race-day results.
