Most marathon runners don’t make it to race day because the real challenges lie in the training, not the race itself.

Common pitfalls include:

- Poor planning and unrealistic expectations  
- Inconsistent training habits  
- Pushing too hard too quickly, leading to injury and burnout  
- Neglecting recovery and rest days  
- Increasing mileage too rapidly without a solid base  
- Overlooking nutrition and hydration  
- Underestimating the mental demands of long-distance running  

Success in marathon training comes down to building steady, consistent habits over time. Regular, manageable runs combined with proper recovery, balanced nutrition, and mental preparation set the foundation for race day.

Discipline in daily training, more than motivation on race morning, makes the difference. Planning wisely and pacing progress give runners the best chance to cross the finish line strong.
