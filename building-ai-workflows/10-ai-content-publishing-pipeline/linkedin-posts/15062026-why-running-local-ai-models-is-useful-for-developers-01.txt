Working with large language models often means relying on hosted APIs, which offer power and ease of use. But running AI models locally brings unique benefits that every developer should consider.

Running models on your own machine allows you to:

- Gain hands-on understanding of how these models work
- Experiment with prompts and workflows in real time
- Reduce costs during early development by avoiding expensive API calls
- Maintain control over private or sensitive data
- See firsthand how hardware capabilities affect performance

While hosted APIs excel in speed, reliability, and access to cutting-edge models—making them ideal for production—local models provide a valuable space for learning and experimentation.

Starting locally helps build intuition and refine approaches before scaling up, ensuring better outcomes throughout the AI development process. Both local and cloud-based models have their place depending on your project’s needs.
