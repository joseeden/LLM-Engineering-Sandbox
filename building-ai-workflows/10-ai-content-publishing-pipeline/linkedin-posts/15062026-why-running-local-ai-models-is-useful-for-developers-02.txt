More developers are turning to large language models through hosted APIs, and for good reason. These services offer powerful AI without heavy setup. 

However, running AI models locally brings unique advantages:

- You gain a hands-on understanding of how models respond to different prompts.
- Experimentation becomes faster and more flexible without repeated network calls.
- Costs are lower during prototyping since you avoid per-request fees.
- Handling sensitive or private data is safer when everything stays on your device.
- Testing on various hardware reveals trade-offs between model size, speed, and accuracy.

That said, hosted APIs remain the best fit for production due to their reliability and scale.

Running AI locally doesn't replace cloud-based solutions—it complements them. For developers eager to deepen their AI knowledge and refine ideas before scaling, local models are an invaluable tool.
