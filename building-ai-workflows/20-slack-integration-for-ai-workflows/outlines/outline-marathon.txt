Title: Why Consistency Matters More Than Speed in Marathon Training

Angle:
Many runners focus too much on pace and distance, but long-term consistency is often the biggest factor in successfully completing a marathon.

Key ideas:

* Marathon training is built over months, not weeks.
* Missing occasional workouts is less harmful than giving up completely.
* Easy runs play an important role in building endurance.
* Recovery is an essential part of training.
* Gradual progress reduces the risk of injury.
* Consistent training habits often produce better results than occasional hard efforts.
